Choropleth Map

This map shows data of indicator 15.4.1, the Average proportion of Mountain Key Biodiversity Areas (KBAs) covered by protected areas (%). This is a part of SDGs goal 15: Life on Land. One of the targets is target 15.4, ENSURE CONSERVATION OF MOUNTAIN ECOSYSTEMS. The UN website explains, “By 2030, ensure the conservation of mountain ecosystems, including their biodiversity, in order to enhance their capacity to provide benefits that are essential for sustainable development.”

A choropleth map shows the distribution of data representing geographic space in colors or patterns. It helps readers to analyze the geographic distribution of data. However, the data representing the area can change depending on the grouping of areas on the map. Therefore, this method is useful to see broad distribution but is weak at spotting specific points in detail.

Making this map started with importing data from websites and cleaning the data. To make a Choropleth map, I need to save the data in different layers. Learning the skills to design and modify the map was easy when I knew what kind of data and folders I was handling and what kind of commands I was giving the computer.
